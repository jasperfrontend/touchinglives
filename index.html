Cornelis Jansen

Cornelis was finally able to succesfully communicate with the parents of his daughter's friend

I visited a Syrian refugee living in my town. His name is Abdul and he’s the father of two children who go to the same school as my children. He barely speaks Dutch and no English and I really wanted to get in touch with him, to get connected, hear his story and to see if I could help him. Well, I now can tell from my very own experience that the device really does make a difference.

At first, the meeting was a bit awkward, with me sitting on his couch, trying to talk through a device. He clearly didn’t feel at ease. But after 15 mins or so, we got used to communicating using the Travis, and then he began to tell his story, why he escaped Syria, what he has seen (and what his oldest daughter has seen), and how they’re doing now. I was really touched and very happy to be able to really get to the heart of matters.

Later on he asked my to help with some documentation, and again with help from Travis, I could. It was very good for me to experience this myself.

Felix Ellwanger

The story of a young Hungarian boy with a rather troublesome background who is pretty torn between the worlds.

"My current “project” is a young Hungarian boy with a rather troublesome background and he is pretty torn between the worlds. English is easy to learn and it’s also a pretty tolerant language when it comes to how to use grammar correctly when speaking to each other. German and Hungarian are the total opposite in this regard.

In German, if you only got one word wrong, the whole sentence sounds weird for a native speaker, which makes it extremely difficult to get really fluent. Hungarians on the other hand have a very strange system of building sentences – it’s on 5th place of the most difficult languages to learn, I think. So besides his personal problems, I have a boy who left Hungary at 9 (so he didn’t learn Hungarian properly), came to Germany, did some language courses in elementary school (together with immigrants from about 10 other mostly Arabic and African countries – which was really “useful”) and now in comprehensive school he’s got English on top. Therefore, now at 12, he has three languages he cannot speak and write properly – this is quite a challenge I can tell you. The Travis device is welcomed here as a fast and mostly reliable thing to get words and phrases translated quickly."
